## Design Context

### Users
Fans of Honkai: Star Rail who are deeply invested in the Amphoreus/Kremnos storyline. They come to read character stories, browse dialogue, explore lore documents, and appreciate gallery art. They are reading-focused, often bilingual (Chinese/English), and value narrative depth over flashy presentation. The site serves as a personal archive — a quiet place to revisit and study the world of Castrum Kremnos.

### Brand Personality
**Classical · Dignified · Tranquil** (古典·庄重·沉静)

The site should feel like stepping into an ancient library — the Garbaniphoro Grand Library itself. Not performative or attention-seeking, but quietly authoritative. The emotional goal is reverence: for the stories, for the characters, for the weight of a fictional history that spans millennia.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Primary reference:** Museum and archive websites (British Museum, Getty Museum, national archives). Academic gravitas, generous whitespace, refined serif typography, content treated as precious artifact.

**Visual language:**
- Warm parchment and linen tones, terracotta/burnt sienna accents (Terra), dark sepia/amber (Gold)
- Cinzel for architectural headings, Noto Serif SC for Chinese body text, Cormorant Garamond for editorial accents
- Grayscale imagery that reveals color on interaction — the archive "coming alive"
- Deliberate, generous spacing — let content breathe like a museum exhibit

**Anti-references (must avoid):**
- AI-generated aesthetics: gradient text, glowing cards, dark mode with neon accents, glassmorphism, SaaS hero layouts
- Commercial game sites: flashy animations, conversion-focused CTAs, promotional banners, particle effects
- The site should never feel like it's selling something or trying to impress with technology

### Design Principles

1. **Content as artifact.** Every piece of text, image, and data is a museum exhibit. Present it with appropriate reverence, spacing, and framing — never crammed or decorated for its own sake.

2. **Quiet authority.** The design should recede so the content speaks. No element should shout for attention. Hierarchy comes from proportion and spacing, not from color intensity or animation.

3. **Generous negative space.** Whitespace is not emptiness — it is the silence between words in a library. Use it deliberately to create rhythm, pacing, and breathing room between sections.

4. **Warmth without excess.** The palette is warm (parchment, terracotta, sepia) but restrained. Accents guide the eye; they don't compete with content. One accent color per context is enough.

5. **Honest materiality.** Favor textures and references rooted in the physical world — paper, stone, linen, ink — over digital effects. Hover states, transitions, and interactions should feel natural, never flashy.
